Cookies Policy

Last Updated: June 3, 2026

This Cookies Policy clarifies how CustomWheel (customwheel.github.io) utilizes localized browser technologies to maintain configurations during user application interactions.

1. What Are Cookies and Local Sessions?

HTTP cookies are small strings of textual records loaded onto terminal equipment when viewing websites. Modern browser environments also offer robust mechanisms such as LocalStorage and SessionStorage. CustomWheel focuses on utilizing modern local browser structures instead of active, persistent third-party tracking scripts.

2. How We Use Browser Storage

Our application uses localized configurations solely to preserve functional preferences while building custom entry parameters. For example, if you alter a color palette theme option, select an active classroom or number preset, or keep an index history of active winner logs during a continuous live stream spin session, these details are stored completely within your local browser storage instance.

3. No Behavioral Tracking Cookies

Because CustomWheel values user transparency, we do not deploy commercial marketing tracking beacons or persistent behavioral cookies across external platforms. Your interface preferences are non-identifiable and never shared with analytics networks or data aggregators.

4. Managing Local Infrastructure

If you prefer to clear or reject localized session memories, you can comfortably configure your native web browser options to drop LocalStorage parameters automatically, block standard assets, or purge cached data when finishing a session. Note that doing so simply means your customized list entries will reset to the original default presets upon reloading the page.
